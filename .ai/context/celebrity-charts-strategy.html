The Concept

Each celebrity gets a permanent, public page on astromi.ai/charts/{name} showing their full natal chart with live transits overlaid in real-time. These pages update automatically — every day the transit positions shift, the TII (Transit Intensity Index) recalculates, and the AI generates a fresh "cosmic weather" reading for that person.

Users can follow any celebrity chart and get notified when something major happens astrologically (Saturn return, eclipse on their Sun, etc.). This creates a reason to come back daily — "What's happening in Taylor Swift's chart today?" — and a natural funnel to sign up and track their own chart.

This strategy targets millions of monthly searches for "[celebrity name] birth chart" and "[celebrity name] astrology" — high-intent queries from people already interested in astrology.

Selection Criteria

Search Volume

These 10 people collectively generate tens of millions of Google searches monthly. Celebrity birth chart queries follow a long-tail pattern — "taylor swift birth chart" alone gets ~200K+ monthly searches, and every related query ("taylor swift zodiac," "taylor swift astrology," "taylor swift rising sign") adds volume.

Demographic Coverage

The list covers key audiences: Gen-Z (Billie, Bad Bunny, Sabrina), Millennials (Taylor, Rihanna, Beyoncé), older demos (Trump, Musk), sports fans (Ronaldo, Messi), and global markets (Latin America, Europe, Asia). Every user segment has someone they care about.

News Cycle Synergy

These are people who generate constant news. Every time Trump makes a policy move, or Taylor drops an album, or Messi scores — there's a spike in searches, and Astromi's transit page can offer an astrological angle. "Here's what was happening in Taylor's chart when she announced..."

Data Quality

7 of 10 have reliable birth times (verified or well-reported). This means accurate Ascendant, houses, and transit timing — which makes the live chart feature genuinely valuable and accurate, not just a gimmick.
